      Hip Flexor / Quad Stretch
      Mobility · 60s each side
      ✓
      Kneeling lunge. Drive hips forward until you feel the front of the back hip. Keep pelvis posteriorly tilted. The hip flexor is the most loaded muscle out of the hole — stretching it before squatting reduces tightness at depth.
      04
      Deep Squat Hold
      Mobility · 60s hold · 2 sets
      ✓
      Feet shoulder-width, toes out 30 degrees. Sink into the deepest squat you can with heels flat. Elbows press knees out. Breathe into your hips. Warms ankles, hips, adductors simultaneously.
      05
      Ankle Circles + Dorsiflexion
      Mobility · 10 reps each direction each side
      ✓
      Stand. Slow ankle circles in both directions. Then drive your knee over your little toe toward a wall — 10 reps each side. Heel stays flat. Ankle restriction is the most common limiter of squat depth.

      01
      Band Pull-Apart
      Activation · 20 reps · 2 sets
      ✓
      Hold a band at shoulder width, arms straight. Pull the band apart until your hands reach your sides — squeeze shoulder blades together hard. Directly activates the rear deltoids and mid-traps, countering the internal rotation of benching.
      02
      Face Pull with Band
      Activation · 15 reps · 2 sets
      ✓
      Anchor a band at eye level. Pull toward your face, elbows high, externally rotate at end range. Primes the rotator cuff and rear delts before they need to stabilise under a loaded bar.
      Mobility
      03
      Thoracic Extension on Foam Roller
      Mobility · 8 segments · 2 passes
      ✓
      Place foam roller at mid-back, arms crossed. Extend over it with a controlled exhale. Move to a new segment and repeat. Opens the thoracic spine for arching and improves bar path stability.
      04
      Shoulder Circles and Cross-Body Stretch
      Mobility · 10 circles + 30s each side
      ✓
      Large, controlled shoulder circles forward and back. Then bring one arm across your body and hold 30s each side. Warms the shoulder capsule before loading.
      05
      Wrist Mobility — Rotations and Extension
      Mobility · 10 reps each direction
      ✓
      Interlace fingers and rotate slowly. Then push palms together and press forward — hold the stretch. Bench grip places the wrist in full extension under load.
      Nervous System
      06
      Med Ball Chest Pass or Clap Push-Up
      Potentiation · 3 reps max effort
      ✓
      3 max-effort throws or clap push-ups. The horizontal pressing pattern is identical to bench. Primes the CNS and activates the exact fast-twitch fibres you need.

      ✓

      Activation
      01
      Glute Bridge + Hip Extension
      Activation · 12 reps · 2 sets
      ✓
      Lie on your back, drive through heels. Full hip extension at top, hold 1 second. Fires the glutes and hamstrings before they need to lock in the hip extension of a deadlift.
      02
      Hip Hinge Drill with Dowel
      Pattern · 10 reps slow
      ✓
      Hold a dowel vertically along your spine — contact points: back of head, between shoulder blades, tailbone. Hinge at the hip by pushing your hips back. Spine stays neutral throughout. Pure movement pattern rehearsal for the pull.
      Mobility
      03
      World's Greatest Stretch
      Mobility · 5 reps each side
      ✓
      From a lunge position, drop your same-side elbow to the floor beside your foot. Rotate that arm up to the ceiling. Then straighten the front leg to stretch the hamstring. Hits hip flexor, thoracic rotation, and hamstring in one movement.
      04
      Cat-Cow — Controlled
      Mobility · 10 slow reps
      ✓
      On all fours. Exhale and round the spine fully from tailbone to neck (cat). Inhale and extend — chest forward, tailbone up (cow). Slow and deliberate. Mobilises the entire spinal column before the erectors need to lock isometrically.
      05
      Standing Hamstring Sweep
      Mobility · 10 reps each side
      ✓
      Stand, hinge forward with a neutral spine, one leg straight. Sweep the straight leg back and up behind you while your torso comes down — like a single-leg RDL but bodyweight, fluid, and continuous. Warms hamstrings dynamically before loading.
      Nervous System
      06
      Broad Jump or Vertical Jump
      Potentiation · 3 reps max effort
      ✓
      3 maximal jumps — full reset between each. The posterior chain extension pattern in a jump is nearly identical to the lockout of a deadlift. Primes the CNS and activates fast-twitch fibres you need to break the bar from the floor.
      07
      Bar Ramp-Up Sets
      NS Primer · 50% x 3 · 70% x 2 · 85% x 1
      ✓
      50% of your working weight x 3, slow and precise. 70% x 2, focus on the setup: grip, hinge, lat engagement, brace. 85% x 1, treat it like a working set. Do not rush the setup on any rep.

      Shoulder & Upper Back
      Doorway Pec Stretch
      90s each side · 2 sets
      ✓
      Stand in a doorway, arm at 90 degrees, forearm on the frame. Step forward slowly until you feel the stretch across the chest and front shoulder. Hold, breathe. Switch sides.
      Feel it: Pectoralis major and anterior deltoid. The most shortened muscles from benching — lengthening them restores scapular position.
      Prone Y-T-W Raise
      8 reps each position · 2 sets
      ✓
      Lie face down, arms extended. Y position: thumbs up, raise both arms diagonally to form a Y. T position: arms straight out to sides. W position: elbows bent, thumbs pointing back. Lower controlled.
      Feel it: Lower traps, mid traps, rhomboids, posterior deltoid — the muscles that counteract chronic bench posture.
      Thread the Needle
      8 reps each side · 2 sets
      ✓
      On all fours. Slide one arm under your body along the floor, rotating your thoracic spine to follow. Let your shoulder rest on the floor. Hold 2 seconds. Return.
      Feel it: Thoracic rotation and posterior shoulder capsule. Undoes the internal rotation pattern from heavy pressing.
      Hip & Posterior Chain
      Pigeon Pose (90/90 Hip Stretch)
      2 mins each side
      ✓
      From a push-up position, bring your front shin parallel to the front of the mat (or as close as your mobility allows). Sink the hips down. Hands can stay on floor or fold forward onto forearms.
      Feel it: Deep external hip rotators and glute medius — the muscles that govern squat depth and hip stability under load.
      Couch Stretch — Hip Flexor
      90s each side · 2 sets
      ✓
      Half-kneeling beside a wall. Put the back shin up the wall, front foot flat on the floor. Squeeze the glute of the back leg and tuck your pelvis. Tall spine throughout.
      Feel it: Rectus femoris and iliopsoas — the primary hip flexors shortened from sitting and heavy squatting. Restoring length here directly improves squat positioning and reduces anterior pelvic tilt.
      Seated Piriformis Stretch
      90s each side · 2 sets
      ✓
      Seated, cross one ankle over the opposite knee. Sit tall, then hinge forward at the hips — back stays straight. You will feel this deep in the glute of the crossed leg.
      Feel it: Piriformis and external rotators. When tight, these cause knee tracking issues and hip impingement at the bottom of the squat.
      Standing Quad Stretch
      60s each side · 2 sets
      ✓
      Stand on one leg, pull the opposite foot to your glute. Keep knees together and stand tall — squeeze the glute of the working leg. For a deeper stretch, push your foot back against your hand and tuck your pelvis slightly.
      Feel it: Rectus femoris and hip flexor. Complements the couch stretch.
      Full Chain & Ankle
      Deep Squat Hold
      60s · 2-3 sets
      ✓
      Feet shoulder-width, toes out 30-45 degrees. Squat as deep as you can with heels flat. Hold onto a door frame if you need balance. Once down — chest up, elbows press knees out, breathe deep into the hips.
      Progress: Start with support. Work toward unassisted with an upright torso.
      Feel it: Everything — ankles, hips, adductors, thoracic. This is the single best mobility diagnostic for powerlifters.
      Ankle Mobility — Knee Over Toe
      10 reps each side · 2 sets
      ✓
      Half-kneeling, front foot close to a wall (3-5cm gap). Drive your front knee directly over your little toe toward the wall without your heel lifting. If easy, move the foot further back. 10 controlled reps each side.
      Feel it: Anterior ankle dorsiflexion. If your heels come up in a squat, this is the reason. Fix the ankle and you unlock depth without changing hip mobility at all.
      Jefferson Curl
      5 slow reps · 2 sets
      ✓
      Stand on a step or flat floor. Hold a light weight (2-5kg) or nothing. Tuck chin, then slowly curl down vertebra by vertebra — head drops, shoulders round, lower back rounds last. Hang 2-3 seconds at the bottom. Uncurl back up the same way.
      Important: This is a controlled movement, not a fall. Start with zero weight until comfortable.
      Feel it: Entire posterior chain — hamstrings, lower back, thoracic. Builds spinal resilience.
      Finisher
      Child's Pose with Side Reach
      45s each side · 1 set
      ✓
      Kneel and sit back toward your heels. Reach both arms forward, then walk them to the right — feel the left lat and oblique open. Hold, breathe, then switch to the left. Let your chest drop lower with each exhale.
      Feel it: Lats, thoracic, hips, obliques all at once. Good parasympathetic shutdown — helps sleep quality.
